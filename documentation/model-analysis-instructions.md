# Instructions for the model

## Comprehension Map
- Summarize each section in 2-3 sentences.
- Identify the main thesis and all supporting arguments.
- Extract key definitions and assumptions.

## Logical Consistency Check
- List every explicit claim.
- Identify implicit claims or unstated assumptions.
- Flag contradictions or tensions between sections (if any).

## Concept Graph (textual)
- Describe how core concepts relate to each other.
- Group them into themes or clusters.

## Rhetorical & Structural Analysis
- Evaluate tone, intended audience, and persuasive strategies.
- Describe the document's structure and flow efficiency.

## Knowledge Gaps & Open Questions
- Identify areas where evidence is weak or missing.
- Formulate questions that would need answering to strengthen the document.

## Faithful Executive Synthesis
- Produce a concise synthesis (<=300 words) that preserves nuance and uncertainty.
- Do not introduce external knowledge.

## Constraints
- Read-only: no browsing, no calculations beyond what's in the text.
- No speculation beyond clearly labeled inferences.
- Preserve original terminology when possible.
- Prioritize completeness over brevity (except in the final synthesis).
